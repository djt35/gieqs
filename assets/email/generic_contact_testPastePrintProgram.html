GIEQs Conference plan for Dr John Anderson

Session : Optimal Colonoscopy Technique (09:00:00 - 10:00:00)
This session covers the focus of what the majority of us do everyday. It is a broad session which will cover a lot of ground allowing more focussed sessions later in the day. Painless intubation, maximal lesion detection on withdrawal and polypectomy using evidence-based, safe and effective techniques.

Role : Live Case

09:00:00 - 09:20:00 : Quality in colonoscopy insertion technique
A masterclass in colonoscopy insertion technique from one of the world experts performed on a patient under sedation. For patients under general anaesthetic these techniques increase safety and time to complete intubation.

Session : Advanced colonoscopy techniques (10:00:00 - 10:45:00)
Following from the previous session we delve into some detail about how to better perform quality colonoscopy. Much of colonoscopy is predictable. But it helps if you know, in advance, if a problem will occur and what you should do to prevent it or rapidly address it if it does occur. We cover the concept of conscious competence, how to identify and remove a loop quickly and painlessly, techniques to perform rapid intubation and complete extubation. All backed up by a wealth of cases and discussion.

Role : Lecture

10:15:00 - 10:30:00 : The commonest loops at colonoscopy. How to prevent them and how to remove them if they occur
Non-progression at colonoscopy can be related to formation of loops. If the colonoscope is not straight this causes discomfort for patients and means that movements made by the endoscopist do not translate into direct movement at the tip, making therapy or lesion detection much more difficult. Here we present an approach to detection and prevention of loops

Session : Gastrointestinal Bleeding Symposium (11:00:00 - 12:00:00)
Too often our management of gastrointestinal bleeding is based on studies that are many years old. We have learned a lot over the last decade from the interventional endoscopy sphere. This session is the result. An update on the most recent best-practice in managing UGIB.

Role : Moderator

Session : Artificial Intelligence in Endoscopy (15:30:00 - 16:30:00)
Whether we like it or not artificial intelligence (AI) in endoscopy is here to stay. We explore the topic from different angles including where the responsibility for patient decision making lies in this new world.

Role : Moderator

Session : Principles of Endoscopy Unit Design and Management (16:30:00 - 17:00:00)
Quality in endoscopy depends on many factors beyond the individual endoscopist. Particularly the design of the endoscopy unit - its protocols, procedures, disinfection facilities, patient preparation, sedation and recovery facilities and the layout of the building. This session attempts to draw from the experience of multiple countries to present a best-practice approach to endoscopy unit design and development.

Role : Moderator

Thu 08 Oct 2020

Session : Cold snare polypectomy (10:10:00 - 10:40:00)
Cold snare polypectomy has demonstrated equivalent efficacy for the resection of small and diminutive colorectal polyps versus hot snare polypectomy. Furthermore it has a zero risk of perforation and extremely low risk of delayed bleeding.

Role : Moderator

Session : Training the future endoscopist (13:45:00 - 14:15:00)
Due to the pressure of our working lives, training is often the last priority. Here we discuss ways to improve training via a structured approach so that even the shortest interaction between trainer and trainee can be fruitful

Role : Moderator

Session : Advanced colonoscopy and polypectomy (14:15:00 - 14:45:00)
Preparation, accessories, caps, timing and stalk manipulation. Usefulness of stalk manipulation prior to the resection of larger pedunculated polyps

Role : Live Case

14:30:00 - 14:45:00 : Pedunculated polypectomy
Patient positioning, how to deal with large stalks, method for looping and transecting the polyp safely

Session : Endoscopic dilatation and stenting (16:15:00 - 17:00:00)
How to dilate safely and effectively, when to select a stent rather than serial dilatation

Role : Moderator

Programme subject to change without notice. We will reconfirm sessions with you nearer the time.
